# 🌸 Saku Bloom Calendar

*A calm digital garden where your effort grows like a tree.*

Saku (咲く) means **“to bloom”** in Japanese.  
This project transforms daily work and goals into a **living cherry blossom tree** that grows as you show up each day.

Instead of treating productivity like a checklist, **Saku treats it like a garden**.

Every action becomes a petal.  
Every streak becomes a branch.  
Every goal eventually blossoms.

---

## 🌿 The Idea

Most productivity tools focus on **tasks and pressure**.

Saku focuses on **growth and consistency**.

> “A tree does not measure its growth.  
> It simply continues reaching upward.”

Your work becomes part of a **visual ecosystem** that evolves over time.

---

## 🌳 How It Works

Every day you can:

- 🌿 Mark that you **worked**
- ✉ Record that you **applied to jobs**
- ✍ Write a **daily reflection**
- 📌 Add **events or tasks**

Each worked day contributes to the **growth of your cherry blossom tree**.

The tree evolves through **six life stages**:

1. 🌱 Seed  
2. 🌿 Sprout  
3. 🌳 Sapling  
4. 🌸 Budding  
5. 🌺 Blooming  
6. 🌸 Full Blossom

When your monthly goal is completed, the garden celebrates your effort.

---

## 🌼 Features

### 📅 Living Calendar

A minimalist monthly calendar to track:

- Worked days
- Job applications
- Events and tasks
- Daily reflections

---

### 🌸 Cherry Blossom Tree Growth

Your effort directly evolves the tree through **six growth phases**.

The tree gently sways in the wind, symbolizing slow and natural progress.

---

### 🔥 Streak Tracking

The garden tracks your **current streak of worked days** to maintain momentum.

---

### 🌿 Monthly Goal System

Set a monthly goal for how many days you want to work.  
Watch your progress bloom as you approach it.

---

### 🍃 Nature Soundscape

Optional ambient nature sounds create a calm environment while planning and reflecting.

---

### 🪷 Sakura Petal Atmosphere

Soft cherry blossom petals drift across the screen to create a peaceful workspace.

---

### 🌸 Goal Celebration

When your goal is completed, the garden celebrates with a **full bloom ceremony**.
